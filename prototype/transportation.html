Getting Here & Around

Everything you need to know about reaching Taniti and exploring the island.

Getting to Taniti

By Air

  • ✈
    Taniti Airport Most visitors arrive by air. The island's small airport currently accommodates small jets and propeller planes. An expansion project is underway to allow larger jets to land, improving access from major international airports.

By Sea

  • 🚢
    Cruise Ship Port Taniti's safe harbor at Yellow Leaf Bay welcomes a small but growing number of cruise ships. The port is located near Merriton Landing with easy access to shops and restaurants.

Getting Around the Island

🚌

Public Buses

Affordable public buses run throughout the island from 5:00 AM to 11:00 PM daily. Routes connect all major tourist areas including Merriton Landing, Taniti City, and popular beaches.

🚐

Private Bus Tours

Several private bus companies offer guided tours of the island, covering major attractions, historical sites, and scenic viewpoints.

🚗

Taxis

Taxis are available throughout the island and can be hailed on the street or called by phone. A convenient option for getting to and from the airport.

🚘

Rental Cars

Rental car agencies are located near the airport and in Merriton Landing. A great option for exploring the island at your own pace.

🚲

Bike Rentals

Explore the island on two wheels with bike rentals available in Merriton Landing. Please note: helmets are required by law for all cyclists.

Transportation Tips

  • 💡
    Plan Ahead for Late Nights Public buses stop running at 11:00 PM. If you plan to enjoy Taniti's nightlife, arrange a taxi in advance for your return trip.
  • ⚠
    Helmet Law Taniti law requires all cyclists to wear helmets. Rental shops typically provide helmets with every bike rental.
  • 💰
    Budget-Friendly Option Public buses are the most affordable way to get around. Consider purchasing a multi-day bus pass if available.
